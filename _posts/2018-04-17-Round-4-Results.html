    Unsurprisingly the five big winners of the round (Collingwood, North Melbourne, Hawthorn, Richmond and West Coast) were the biggest gainers for the round, while their opponents (Adelaide, Carlton, Melbourne, Brisbane and Gold Coast) moved down the furthest.

    Adelaide's big loss to Collingwood saw them fall from the number 1 ranking to number 4, while Richmond moved from the 5th ranked side to the top for the first time this season. Despite an 80 point loss and losing over 4 rating points Gold Coast managaged to move closer to 16th and 17th ranked Brisbane and Carlton on the back of their big losses over the weekend.

    As expected the competition's ratings are spreading further apart after the offseason's regression with the gap between Richmond and Gold Coast now close to 7 goals.
